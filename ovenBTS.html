Oven Baked Tofu Shakshuka

  • 50 mins
  • Main

Shakshuka is known as an easy and healthy breakfast option in many countries, but it can definitely be enjoyed at any time during the day. We gave our own plant-based spin to the classic dish and used silken tofu, which provides a very "eggy" feel to that of the traditional Shakshuka, especially when combined with kala namak salt.

  • Total
  • 50 mins
  • Prep
  • 10 mins
  • Cook
  • 40 min

Ingredients

  • 1⅔ cups (400 g) canned diced tomatoes
  • 2 red bell peppers, chopped
  • 1 medium red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 red hot chili peppers, seeds removed, minced†
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 Tbsp (33 g) tomato paste
  • 1 Tbsp (15 mL) olive oil
  • 1½ tsp (3 g) smoked sweet paprika powder
  • 1 tsp (2 g) ground cumin
  • 1 tsp (2 g) ground coriander
  • 1 tsp (6 g) salt
  • 1 tsp (4 g) kala namak‡
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 11 oz (300 g) silken tofu
  • 5 small pita breads, toasted

Optional Garnish

  • fresh cilantro
  • Zaa'atar spice mix
  • Lemon spice

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 390°F (200°C). To a 9 x 12 inch (23 x 31 cm) baking dish*, add the diced tomato, bell pepper, onion, chilis, garlic, tomato paste, olive oil, paprika, cumin, coriander, and salt.
  2. Mix together, then bake in the oven for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, on a large plate, mix together the kala namak and turmeric. Slice the tofu into even ½ inch (1 cm) thick slices and coat lightly with the salt and turmeric mix.
  3. Place the sliced silken tofu on top of the tomato mixture and press the pieces in a little bit. Transfer the baking dish back into the oven and let it bake for another 10 - 15 minutes.
  4. Serve with toasted pitas, and garnish with za'atar, cilantro, and lemon slices. Enjoy!
