Saucy Beans on Garlic Toast

  • 10 mins
  • Main
  • BreakFast

Navy beans, in the Netherlands go by the descriptive name of "witte bonen" (white beans). They are also known as haricot or Boston beans and are a fantastic base for bold flavours. Inspired by Britain's classic beans on toast recipes, we've added a spicy kick to the usual baked beans. Making the toast into buttery garlic bread adds a burst of flavour to this simple, quick meal. Serving

  • Total
  • 10 mins
  • Prep
  • 5 mins
  • Cook
  • 5 min

Ingredients

Garlic Toast

  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 Tbsp (15 mL) olive oil
  • 2 slices rustic bread

Saucy beans

  • 1⅓ cups (243 g) cooked navy beans
  • ½ cup (66 g) tomato paste
  • ¼ cup (60 mL) water
  • ½ vegetable bouillon cube, crushed
  • 1 Tbsp (15 mL) chipotle adobo sauce
  • 2 tsp (10 mL) maple syrup
  • 1 tsp (2 g) ground cumin
  • ½ tsp onion powder

Toppings

  • ¼ cup (60 mL) unsweetened soy Greek-style yogurt
  • 2 Tbsp (19 g) pickled jalapeños , sliced

Directions

  1. Add the garlic and oil to a small bowl. Stir.
  2. Use a silicone pastry brush to brush the garlic oil onto both sides of the bread. Transfer the bread to a large pan on medium heat and allow to toast until lightly golden on both sides.
  3. To a small pan on medium heat, add the ingredients for the "saucy beans". Cook for 5 minutes, or until heated through.
  4. Spoon the beans over the garlic toast and serve with a dollop of yogurt and some pickled jalapeño if desired. Enjoy!
